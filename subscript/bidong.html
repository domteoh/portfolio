The Waves Now Calm

XX November 2024 | Film released in 2020


Pulau Bidong is a small island, but it sits at the center of a venn diagram where local, regional and diasporic identities overlap.

About 250,000 people passed through the island during the Indochina refugee crisis. Yet, it’s something I never learned about in school and rarely heard people talk about in Malaysia. It was only when I met Khoa, halfway across the world in the United States, that I learned about the island’s history.

This film was our passion project. For me, it was a way to delve into Malaysia’s history and learn more about our role in global resettlement programs. For Khoa, it was a way to learn about family history – what it means to flee conflict and rebuild a life, but also to question what it means to live the American Dream.

The Indochina refugee crisis may be over, but the term "boat people" persists in other contexts. As governments push boats back to sea and politicians talk of building walls, we hope this film will encourage a bit more empathy towards those fleeing conflict and persecution, and show how well they can do when given the right support.

We’ve been fortunate to screen this film at the SEAxSEA Film Festival and Viet Film Fest – two wonderful initiatives that celebrate Southeast Asian stories and voices — but it’s time we make the film accessible to anyone who may be interested. For those who passed through the island, for their children, for Malaysians eager to learn about their country’s history.
